Region 5 - Bicol Region

Administrative divisions

Bicol Region, often known as Region V, is an administrative area in the Philippines that is part of the Luzon island group. It is divided into six provinces: Albay, Camarines Norte, Camarines Sur, Catanduanes, Masbate, and Sorsogon. The city of Legazpi serves as the regional hub.

There are six provinces in the region: Albay, Camarines Norte, Camarines Sur, Sorsogon, and the island provinces of Catanduanes and Masbate, as well as 107 municipalities and 3,471 barangays.

History of Bicol Region

The Bicol area was known as Ibalong, a perversion of Gibal-ong, a sitio of Magallanes, Sorsogon where the Spaniards first landed in 1567. In 1572, the Bicol River was first referenced in Spanish texts. Los Camarines was also named after the cottages discovered by the Spaniards at Camalig, Albay. Bicol has no prehistoric animal fossils, thus the region's population history is unknown. The Aeta from Camarines Sur to Sorsogon clearly imply that aborigines were there a long time ago, but the earliest evidence dates from the middle to late Neolithic period.

By 1569, there was a barangay (village) system in place. Documents provide no evidence of Islamic dominance or any authority greater than the datu (chieftain). Strength, courage, and intellect were the pillars of precolonial leadership. The native seems uninterested in politics. As a result, the datu's influence was most important during times of crisis, such as wartime. Otherwise, early Bicol society was based on the family, and the leader was the head of the household.

The efforts of Augustinian and Franciscan Spanish missionaries were primarily responsible for the Spanish impact in Bicol. The Holy Order of the Franciscans founded the first churches in Bicol, the San Francisco Church and the Naga Cathedral in Naga City, as well as the Holy Cross Parish in Nabua, Camarines Sur. The Archdiocese of Caceres, one of the Philippines' oldest dioceses, was likewise established in the Bicol Region. At this period, Bicol was dotted with several astilleros (shipyards) that were focused on constructing Manila Galleons, the largest ships in their day, from the indigenous hardwood trees, and these Manila Galleons were responsible for commerce between Asia and the Americas.

Demographics

As of the 2020 census, the population of Bicol Region was 6,082,165, expanding at a 1.29 percent annual rate between 2010 and 2015. In 2015, the region's population density climbed to 320 people per square kilometer.

Camarines Sur is the region's largest province in terms of area and population as of 2020, encompassing 5,481.6 km2 (2,116.5 sq mi) or around 30.4% of total land area with a population of 2,068,244. Catanduanes is the smallest in both size and population, covering just 1,511.5 km2 (583.6 sq mi) or 8.4% of the total regional area and housing 271,879 people.

The Bicolanos are descended from Austronesian-speaking immigrants who arrived in the Iron Age from Southern China. Many Bicolanos have Chinese, Arab, and Spanish ancestors. The majority of the residents are of Spanish descent, and their language is known as Bikol or Bikolano. Bicolanos have a high rate of Spanish introgression, with a government-sponsored research revealing that two out of every ten Bicolanos, or two-tenths of the population, are of Spanish heritage. Only the Chavacanos have a higher Spanish percentage among Philippine ethnic groups, at 4/10ths of the population, compared to the Bicolanos' 2/10ths,[13], while the average percentage of Spanish descent among other Filipino Lowland Christian Ethnic groups is 6% out of 100%.[14] The Bicolano language is very fragmented, and its dialects are mutually incomprehensible.

Region V

Bicol, known formally as the Bicol Region or colloquially as Bicolandia (Central Bikol: Rehiyon kan Bikol; Rinconada Bikol: Rehiyon ka Bikol; Waray Sorsogon, Masbateño: Rehiyon san Bikol; Tagalog: Rehiyon ng Bikol), is an administrative region of the Philippines, designated as Region V. Bicol comprises six provinces, four on the Bicol Peninsula mainland (the southeastern end of Luzon) – Albay, Camarines Norte, Camarines Sur, and Sorsogon – and the offshore island provinces of Catanduanes and Masbate.[3] The regional center is Legazpi City and has one Independent Component City, the pilgrim city of Naga.[4][3] The region is bounded by the Lamon Bay to the north, the Philippine Sea to the east, and the Sibuyan Sea and Ragay Gulf to the west. The northernmost provinces, Camarines Norte and Camarines Sur, are bordered to the west by the province of Quezon.
