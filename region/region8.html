History of Eastern Visayas

Eastern Visayas is one of two areas in the Philippines that do not share a physical boundary with another, the other being MIMAROPA, and is classified as Region VIII. Its six provinces, Biliran, Eastern Samar, Leyte, Northern Samar, Samar, and Southern Leyte, are among the poorest in the country. The economy is mostly based on agriculture. Some of their agricultural techniques trace back to the early 1800s in various sections of the region. Because of the traditionally rocky interior of the two main islands, agricultural growth has been gradual and very selective to a few pockets of more ambitious and well-off farmers. Agricultural has generally been restricted to coastal regions and tiny island valleys.

Heavy industry has been restricted to a tiny industrial zone on the northwest coast of Leyte Island, and most of the hinterland, particularly in Samar, has recovered from the disastrous logging activities of the 1970s and 1980s. After his lengthy expedition across the Pacific in 1521, the legendary Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan first saw the landing on Samar and Leyte. The residents of Limasawa, where the first mass was held, were the first Christians in Southeast Asia. Five decades later, General Douglas MacArthur led the Allies to victory in the Battle of Leyte, which was followed by the Battle of Leyte Gulf. Leyte became the Allies' safe base in capturing the Philippines.

Demographics

According to the 2020 Census of Population and Housing, the population of Region VIII - Eastern Visayas as of May 1, 2020 is 4,547,150. (2020 CPH). In 2020, this will account for around 4.17 percent of the Filipino population.

Languages

Abaknon is a dialect spoken on Capul Island in Northern Samar.
Baybayanon is a dialect spoken in Baybay, Leyte.
Boholano is a language spoken in Southern Leyte.
Cebuano is a language spoken in Biliran, Leyte, Southern Leyte, Samar, Northern Samar, and Eastern Samar. It is the primary language in those locations.
Kinabalian is a dialect spoken in San Juan, Southern Leyte.
Waray-Waray is an indigenous language spoken in Biliran, Leyte, Southern Leyte, Samar, Northern Samar, and Eastern Samar. It is the regional language.

Region VIII

Eastern Visayas (Waray: Sinirangan Kabisay-an; Cebuano: Sidlakang Kabisay-an; Tagalog: Silangang Kabisayaan or Silangang Visayas) is an administrative region in the Philippines, designated as Region VIII. It consists of three main islands, Samar, Leyte and Biliran. The region has six provinces, one independent city and one highly urbanized city[3] namely, Biliran, Leyte, Northern Samar, Samar, Eastern Samar, Southern Leyte, Ormoc and Tacloban. The highly urbanized city of Tacloban is the sole regional center. These provinces and cities occupy the easternmost islands of the Visayas group of islands.
