Region 6 - Western Visayas

Administrative divisions

Western Visayas (Hiligaynon: Kabisay-an Nakatundan; Tagalog: Kanlurang Kabisayaan or Kanlurang Visayas) is a Philippine administrative area that includes six provinces (Aklan, Antique, Capiz, Guimaras, Iloilo, and Negros Occidentales) and two highly urbanized cities (Bacolod and Iloilo City). Iloilo City serves as the regional hub.

History of Western Visayas

The provinces of the Philippines were restructured into 11 regions by Presidential Decree No. 1 on September 24, 1972, as part of President Ferdinand Marcos' Integrated Reorganization Plan. The Western Visayas area was formed of the provinces of Aklan, Antique, Capiz, Iloilo (with its then-subprovince of Guimaras), and Negros Occidentales.

The province of Palawan was transferred to Region VI (Western Visayas) by Executive Order 429 on May 23, 2005. The Department of Interior and Local Government announced in June 2005 that the transfer had been completed. However, Palaweos criticized the move, citing a lack of consultation, with most residents in Puerto Princesa City and all municipalities except one preferring to remain in Region IV-B. This Order instructed the suspension of Executive Order 429 awaiting the development of an implementation plan for the orderly transfer of Palawan from Region IV-B to Region VI. As a result, Palawan remains in Region IV-B for the time being.

By virtue of Executive Order No. 183 issued on May 29, 2015, by President Benigno Aquino III, the province of Negros Occidental and its capital, Bacolod, were both removed from Western Visayas in order to form the Negros Island Region with Negros Oriental. However, on August 9, 2017, President Rodrigo Duterte dissolved the Negros Island Region, revoking Executive Order No. 1. According to Benjamin Diokno, the Secretary of Budget and Management, the signing of Executive Order No. 38 in 2015 cited a lack of money to completely implement the NIR.

Demographics

According to the 2020 Census of Population and Housing, the population of Region VI - Western Visayas as of May 1, 2020 is 7,954,723 people (2020 CPH). In 2020, this will account for around 7.30 percent of the Filipino population.

Languages

Aklanon/Akeanon is a language spoken in Aklan and northeastern Capiz.

Capiznon is a Capiz language spoken in Capiz, northeastern Iloilo, and eastern Aklan.

Cebuano is a language spoken in the northeastern Negros Occidentales.

Hiligaynon is spoken in the provinces of Iloilo, Negros Occidentales, Guimaras, Capiz, Antique, and Aklan. It is the regional language.

Kinaray-a is the language spoken in Antique, the southwestern portion of Iloilo, Guimaras, western Capiz, and southwestern Aklan.

Malaynon is a language spoken in northern Aklan, including Boracay Island.

Caluyanon is a Semirara Islands language (Semirara, Caluya, and Sibay Islands).

Region VI

Western Visayas (Hiligaynon: Kabisay-an Nakatundan; Tagalog: Kanlurang Kabisayaan or Kanlurang Visayas) is an administrative region in the Philippines, numerically designated as Region VI. It consists of six provinces (Aklan, Antique, Capiz, Guimaras, Iloilo and Negros Occidental) and two highly urbanized cities (Bacolod and Iloilo City). The regional center is Iloilo City. The region is dominated by the native speakers of four Visayan languages: Hiligaynon, Kinaray-a, Aklanon and Capiznon. The land area of the region is 20,794.18 km2 (8,028.68 sq mi), and with a population of 7,954,723 inhabitants, it is the second most populous region in the Visayas after Central Visayas.
