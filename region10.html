Region 10 - Northern Mindanao

Administrative divisions

Bukidnon, the island province of Camiguin, Lanao del Norte, Misamis Occidental, and Misamis Oriental comprise the area. Cagayan de Oro and Iligan, two heavily urbanized cities, are managed independently of any province. The provinces are further split into seven component cities, 84 municipalities, and 2,022 barangays. Cagayan de Oro is the regional center.

History of Northern Mindanao

The region's present name is derived from its location on Mindanao island. Americans formally invented the word during the installation of American colonial power in the Philippines following the defeat of Filipino insurgents. There have been suggestions that the present Northern Mindanao area, which is dominated by the Cebuano ethnic group, be renamed the Amihanan region. Amihanan literally translates to "northern area" in the Cebuano language, which is the region's lingua franca.

CAMIGUIN’S HISTORY

• 1521 and 1565 - Ferdinand Magellan and Miguel Lopez de Legazpi arrived in the • The first Spanish colony in the island was called "Guinsiliban," a Kinamiguin term that means "to watch for pirates from a watchtower." 1901 - American soldiers landed on the island, seizing governmental authority. Valero Camaro led a troop of Camiguingnons in their fight for independence against foreign invasion. Valero Camaro was assassinated and shot in the head. He was an unsung Camiguin patriot during the early independence movement.

• On June 18, 1942, the Japanese Imperial Army arrived in Camiguin and established a government in Mambajao.

• 1945 - At the close of World War II, Philippine Commonwealth forces arrived at Camiguin and liberated the island during the Battle of Camiguin against Japanese Imperial forces.

• Camiguin was part of Misamis Oriental from 1946 to 1958. • It was made a sub-province in 1958.

BUKIDNON'S HISTORY

• 1820 - Bukidnon became a part of Misamis. The entire region was known as Malaybalay, and the population were referred to as "Bukidnon," or mountain dwellers. • Dean C. Worcester, the Philippine Commission's head, recommended separating Bukidnon from Misamis.

• On August 20, 1907, the Philippine Commission Act No. 1693 established the Province of Agusan and the sub-province of Bukidnon. • Under Philippine Act 2711, Bukidnon became a formal province on March 10, 1917.

• 1942 - Japanese troops conquer the province.

1945 - During WWII, Filipino and American soldiers rescued the province from Japanese control with the help of Bukidnon-based Filipino insurgents.

LANAO DEL NORTE'S HISTORY

• Lanao was a province that existed from 1914 until 1959. Lanao was partitioned into two (2) provinces by Republic Act No. 2228, forming Lanao del Norte and Lanao del Sur. On July 4, 1959, the new province was established, with Iligan City as its seat.

• 1977 – President Ferdinand E. Marcos signed Resolution No. 805, series of 1977 of the Sangguniang Panlalawigan (Parliamentary Bill No. 586) sponsored by Assemblyman Abdullah D. Dimaporo into Presidential Decree 181, transferring the province's capital from Iligan City to Tubod.

• Despite the commencement of the MILF conflict in March 2000, peace and order were restored via the efforts of the province government.

HISTORY OF MISAMIS ORIENTAL

• In the 10th century, the territory that is now Misamis Oriental was ruled by the Rajahnate of Butuan, an Indianized kingdom.

• In the 16th century, Muslims from Malaysia arrived and drove the non-Muslim Lumads northwards, gaining control of much of Mindanao and converting the populace to Islam.

• The province was exposed to Moro Raids during the Spanish colonization.

• Ozamiz's Fuerza de la Concepcion y del Triunfo was created as a Spanish military facility and one of the staging locations for their campaigns against Moros.

Demographics

According to the 2015 Philippine Statistics Authority Census, Northern Mindanao is the eighth most populated area in the Philippines and the second most populous region in Mindanao (after Davao Region). Region X's primary metropolitan region is Metro Cagayan de Oro, which encompasses Cagayan de Oro as well as various towns and localities in Misamis Oriental and Bukidnon. The population was 5,022,768 according to the 2020 Census. This accounted for 19.13% of the total population of Mindanao, or 4.61% of the total population of the Philippines.

The great majority of the region's residents are derived from Cebu and Bohol immigration. Other residents include Waray, Hiligaynon, Zamboangueo, Maranao, and Tausug, while the interior highlands are home to the indigenous Bukidnons and Manobos.

The majority religion is Christianity, with Roman Catholicism accounting for 72% of the population and Protestants accounting for 14%. Islam is the second most common religion, accounting for 8% of the population.

Region X

Northern Mindanao (Tagalog: Hilagang Mindanao; Cebuano: Amihanang Mindanao; Maranao: Pangotaraan Mindanao) is an administrative region in the Philippines, designated as Region X. It comprises five provinces: Bukidnon, Camiguin, Misamis Occidental, Misamis Oriental, and Lanao del Norte, and two cities classified as highly urbanized, all occupying the north-central part of Mindanao island, and the island-province of Camiguin. The regional center is Cagayan de Oro. Lanao del Norte was transferred to Northern Mindanao from Region XII (then called Central Mindanao) by virtue of Executive Order No. 36 in September 2001.[3]
