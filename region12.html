Region 12 - Soccsksargen

Administrative divisions

SOCCSKSARGEN, formally known as area XII, is a Philippine administrative area located in the southern-central part of Mindanao. It includes four provinces: Cotabato, Sarangani, South Cotabato, and Sultan Kudarat, as well as one highly urbanized metropolis (General Santos) and Cotabato's separate component city. The City of Koronadal serves as the regional hub.

History of Soccsksargen

The Maitum Anthropomorphic Pottery were discovered at Maitum, Sarangani, the region's earliest culture. The jars have been designated as National Cultural Treasures, and they are subject to the strict safeguards provided by Philippine and international law.

The region was once known as Central Mindanao. Prior to the formation of the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (ARMM), it included the provinces of:
• Maguindanao
• North Cotabato
• Sultan Kudarat
• Lanao del Norte
• Lanao del Sur

Lanao del Sur, Maguindanao (excluding Cotabato City), and Sultan Kudarat were removed from the region with the creation of ARMM, leaving Lanao del Norte and Cotabato, as well as Iligan, Marawi, and Cotabato City as constituent provinces and cities. Lanao del Norte and Iligan were later transferred to Northern Mindanao, while Marawi became part of the ARMM. Sultan Kudarat was moved from ARMM to Central Mindanao on February 23, 1995.

Soccsksargen, President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo signed Executive Order No. 36 in September 2001, shifting South Cotabato, Sarangani, and General Santos (commonly known as the SocSarGen District) from Southern Mindanao to area XII and renamed the area Soccsksargen from Central Mindanao.

Koronadal has been designated as the new regional center
On March 30, 2004, Koronadal was designated as the regional political and socioeconomic hub of Soccsksargen by Executive Order No. 304, issued by then-President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo. Regional agencies, bureaus, and offices were compelled to relocate from Cotabato City, the region's old regional center.

Cotabato City exit and special Cotabato barangays
Despite serving as the administration headquarters of the Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao, the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region establishment referendum on January 21, 2019 resulted in the unexpected confirmation of the Bangsamoro Organic Law in Cotabato City. This implies that the city may now legally function as the region's capital, and the offices of the previous ARMM will be kept for use by the Bangsamoro Regional Government. The city also no longer formally belongs to the Soccsksargen Region. In addition to Cotabato City, 63 barangays in Cotabato province chose to join Bangsamoro, thereby leaving the area.

Region XII

Soccsksargen (officially stylized in all caps;[3] [sokˈsardʒɛn]), formerly known as Central Mindanao, is an administrative region of the Philippines, designated as Region XII. Located in south-central Mindanao, its name is an acronym that stands for the region's four provinces and one highly urbanized city (South Cotabato, Cotabato, Sultan Kudarat, Sarangani and General Santos). The regional center is in Koronadal, located in the province of South Cotabato, and the center of commerce and industry is General Santos, which is the most populous city in the region.
