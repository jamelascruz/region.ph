Region 4B - Mimaropa

Administrative divisions

Mimaropa (typically capitalized in official government papers), also known as the Southwestern Tagalog Region, is a Filipino administrative area. It was also formerly, but still popularly, known as Region IV-B until 2016. It is one of just two areas in the country that does not share a land boundary with another (the other being Eastern Visayas). Mindoro (split into Occidental Mindoro and Oriental Mindoro), Marinduque, Romblon, and Palawan are the provinces that make up the name.

Mimaropa is made up of five provinces, one highly urbanized metropolis (Puerto Princesa), one component city (Calapan), 71 municipalities, and 1,458 barangays.

History of Mimaropa

Mimaropa and Calabarzon were formed with the division of Southern Tagalog (Region IV) into the two regions on May 17, 2002, with the issuance of Executive Order No. 103 by then-President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo; for the purpose of promoting government efficiency, accelerating social and economic development, and improving public services in the provinces covered. Mimaropa, which refers for the island provinces of the Southern Tagalog region—Mindoro (Oriental and Occidental), Marinduque, Romblon, and Palawan—was designated as Region IV-B. Calabarzon was classified as Region IV-A.

On May 23, 2005, Executive Order 429 was issued, relocating Palawan to the area of Western Visayas and naming Region IV-B "Mimaro." Nevertheless, Palawan people challenged the change, alleging a lack of engagement, with the majority of citizens in Puerto Princesa and practically all towns opting to remain in Region IV-B. To address this criticism, Administrative Order No. 129 was issued on August 19, 2005. This directive directed that Executive Order 429 be suspended pending the approval of a plan for the orderly transfer of Palawan from Mimaropa to Region VI.

The region's name was listed as "Mimaropa" in the 2010 Philippine Census of Population, and Palawan was included in the region. It is unclear if the transfer of Palawan to Western Visayas is still deemed pending by the Filipino government as of 2014. As of 2014, the National Statistics Coordination Board of the Philippines still classified Palawan province as part of the Mimaropa area.

On July 17, 2016, Republic Act No. 10879 effectively established the Mimaropa Region in the Southwestern Tagalog Region. This featured no border adjustments but was in effect only a rebranding and discontinuance of the "Region IV-B" title.

Demographics

The population was 3,228,558 according to the 2020 Census. This accounted for 5.19% of the total population of the Luzon island group, or 2.96% of the total population of the Philippines.

Alangan, Tawbuid, and Buhid, are dialect spoken in Mindoro's interior while Asi is a Romblon and Marinduque language.Palawan dialects include Calamian Tagbanwa, Cuyunon, and Hiligaynon.In Mindoro's interior, Hanunoo, Tadyawan, and Iraya are spoken.Southern Palawan is home to the Malay, Molbog, and Tausog languages.

Romblon term for onhan, Palawano is the language of Palawan,Romblomanon is a dialect of Romblon while Romblon is the language of Sibuyanon.

Tagalog is a Filipino language spoken in the Philippines' Occidental and Oriental Mindoro, as well as Marinduque, Romblon, and Palawan. It is the regional lingua franca, largely spoken in Filipino.
