Region 3 - Central Luzon

Administrative divisions

Central Luzon, formally known as Region III, is a Filipino administrative area located in the center of the island of Luzon. It includes seven provinces: Aurora, Bataan, Bulacan, Nueva Ecija, Pampanga, Tarlac, and Zambales, as well as two major cities. The City of San Fernando serves as the regional hub. primarily serves to arrange the seven provinces of the vast central plains of the island of Luzon for administrative convenience.

History of Central Luzon

The region's present name relates to its location on the island of Luzon. With the fall of the First Philippine Republic, US colonialists invented the word. There have been suggestions that the present Central Luzon area be renamed the Luzones region. The suggested name is a play on Luzon island's historical name, Luçes, which was subsequently used to refer to the middle portion of the island, ranging from Pagasinan in the north to Pampanga in the south.The term Luçes literally translates into Luzones.

Central Luzon (Kapampangan: (Reyun ning) Kalibudtarang Luzon, Pangasinan: (Rehiyon na) Pegley na Luzon, Tagalog: (Rehiyon ng) Gitnang Luzon, Ilocano: (Rehion/Deppaar ti) Tengnga ti Luzon), designated as Region III, is an administrative region in the Philippines, primarily serving to organize the 7 provinces of the vast central plains of the island of Luzon (the largest island), for administrative convenience. The region contains the country's largest plain and produces the majority of the country's rice supply, earning it the moniker "Rice Granary of the Philippines."[1] Its provinces are: Aurora, Bataan, Bulacan, Nueva Ecija, Pampanga, Tarlac, and Zambales. Also, the province of Aurora was included.

Demographics

According to the 2020 Census of Population and Housing, the population of Region III - Central Luzon as of May 1, 2020 is 12,422,172. (2020 CPH). In 2020, this will account for around 11.39 percent of the Filipino population.

Tagalogs, Ilocanos, and Kapampangans make up the majority of the population in the region. Indigenous cultural groups including the Dumagats, Aetas or Itas, Igorot, Ilongot, Isinai, Bugkalot, and Zambales live in the hinterlands and mountains.
