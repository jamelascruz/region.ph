Region 7 - Central Visayas

Administrative divisions

The Central Visayas area is made up of four provinces and three autonomous cities. Central Visayas, formally known as Region VII, is a Filipino administrative area that spans the whole island of Visayas. It encompasses four provinces: Bohol, Cebu, Negros Orientales, and Siquijor, as well as three highly urbanized cities. Cebu City serves as the regional hub.

History of Central Visayas

The provinces of the Philippines were restructured into 11 regions by Presidential Decree No. 1 on September 24, 1972, as part of President Ferdinand Marcos' Integrated Reorganization Plan. The Central Visayas area was formed by combining the provinces of Cebu, Bohol, and Negros Oriental (with its then-subprovince of Siquijor).

By virtue of Executive Order No. 183 issued by President Benigno Aquino III on May 29, 2015, the province of Negros Oriental was removed from Central Visayas to form the Negros Island Region along with Negros Occidental and its provincial capital, Bacolod.[5] However, on August 9, 2017, President Rodrigo Duterte dissolved the Negros Island Region, revoking Executive Order No. 183, s. According to Benjamin Diokno, then Secretary of Budget and Management, Obama signed Executive Order No. 38 in2015, claiming a lack of funding to properly implement the NIR.

Demographics

The population was 8,081,988 according to the 2020 Census. This accounted for 39.26% of the Visayas island group's total population, or 7.41% of the Philippines' total population.

Languages

Bantayanon is a Cebu provincial dialect spoken on Bantayan Island.
Boholano is a Cebuano dialect spoken in the island of Bohol.
Cebuano is a language spoken in Cebu, the Negros Orientales, Bohol, and Siquijor. It is the regional language.
Hiligaynon is a language spoken in the western Negros Oriental.
Porohanon is a Cebu province dialect spoken in the Camotes Islands.

Region VII

Central Visayas (Cebuano: Tunga-tungang Kabisay-an; Tagalog: Gitnang Kabisayaan) is an administrative region in the Philippines, numerically designated as Region VII. It consists of four provinces: (Cebu, Bohol, Negros Oriental, and Siquijor) and three highly urbanized cities: Cebu City, Lapu-Lapu, and Mandaue). Major islands are the eponymous Cebu, Bohol, and Siquijor, together with the eastern part of Negros. The regional center and largest city is Cebu City. The region is dominated by the native speakers of four Visayan languages: Cebuano, Bantayanon, Boholano, and Porohanon. The land area of the region is 15,895.66 km2 (6,137.35 sq mi), and with a population of 8,081,988 inhabitants, it is the second most populous region in the Visayas.
